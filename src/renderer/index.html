WIRED / SECURITY · 7 MIN READ

The browser is the last operating system — and it's been compromised for twenty years.

BY S. HARADA APR 17 2026 ARCHIVED · 4 MIRRORS

Every click you have ever made passed through a chain of middlemen who knew more about the tab than you did. Fingerprint brokers, analytics shims, supply-chain scripts embedded four layers deep in a stylesheet — they watch, and the watching is the product.

[ EDITORIAL IMAGE — DROP IN ]

Revenant is a response. The architecture is deliberately adversarial: three independent render lanes, each with its own identity shell, each able to pretend to be a different browser on a different machine in a different country at the same time. Nothing correlates. That is the feature.

For the first time in twenty years, the browser stops being the collection point. It becomes the ghost — reading, recording, returning — and leaving no shadow.

Ternary-Routed Egress for Adversarial Browsing: A Four-Year Deployment Study.

M. IONESCU · R. PARK · S. HARADA APR 2026 128 CITATIONS

We describe a production deployment of a ternary-routed browser egress system serving 412,000 daily active users over a four-year window. Each outbound request is replicated along three cryptographically-independent paths, with quorum-based response selection.

This approach is resistant to single-ISP observation, rogue-node injection, and timing-correlation attacks — at the cost of 2.8× bandwidth overhead and 41ms p50 latency increase, both of which are well within user tolerance as measured by retention.
